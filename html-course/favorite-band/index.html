Nirvana

Nirvana was a highly influential American rock band formed in Aberdeen, Washington in 1987. Fronted by singer and guitarist Kurt Cobain alongside bassist Krist Novoselic and drummer Dave Grohl, the trio became the definitive flagship band of Generation X. They are best known for exploding the grunge music movement into the global mainstream during the early 1990s.

Key Facts:

• The Sound: Fused the raw, aggressive energy of punk rock with catchy, pop-infused melodies.

• Breakthrough Hit: Their 1991 single "Smells Like Teen Spirit" from the album Nevermind knocked Michael Jackson off the top of the charts.

• Essential Albums: Released three studio albums—Bleach (1989), Nevermind (1991), and In Utero (1993).

• The End: The band abruptly dissolved in April 1994 following the tragic death of Kurt Cobain.

• Aftermath: Dave Grohl went on to successfully form and front the rock band, the Foo Fighters.
